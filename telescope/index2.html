James Webb Space Telescope

Explore the cutting-edge components that make up the world’s most powerful space telescope.

Primary Mirror

The Primary Mirror is the centerpiece of JWST. It’s a 6.5-meter segmented mirror made of 18 hexagonal segments. This mirror collects light from distant galaxies and stars, allowing the telescope to see deeper into space than ever before.

Importance: The mirror's size allows for the capture of faint infrared signals from the early universe, helping to study the formation of stars and planets.

Sunshield

The Sunshield is a critical component that protects the telescope from the heat of the Sun and keeps its instruments cool. It is the size of a tennis court and made up of five layers of a special material called Kapton.

Importance: By blocking solar radiation, the Sunshield ensures that the instruments can operate at the necessary temperatures for infrared observations.

Integrated Science Instrument Module (ISIM)

The ISIM houses the four scientific instruments that JWST uses to capture data: NIRCam, NIRSpec, MIRI, and FGS/NIRISS. Each instrument is designed for specific wavelengths and types of observations.

Importance: The ISIM allows JWST to conduct a wide range of astronomical studies, from exoplanets to distant galaxies.

Telescope Structure

The overall Telescope Structure supports all components and maintains the precise alignment needed for optimal imaging. It is designed to withstand the harsh environment of space.

Importance: A stable and robust structure is essential for maintaining the alignment of the mirrors and instruments, ensuring accurate observations.

Reaction Wheels

The Reaction Wheels are responsible for controlling the orientation of JWST in space. They spin to create angular momentum, allowing the telescope to rotate and point at different targets.

Importance: Precise pointing is crucial for capturing clear images and data from distant astronomical objects.
